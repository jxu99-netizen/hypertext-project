If I Were Not an International Student

by Jiarui Xu

I wake up to the sound of traffic outside my dorm.
If I had stayed home, maybe I would still hear my mother calling me for breakfast.

I walk through a campus full of strangers.
At home, I would have known every corner, every name, every tree that bloomed in spring.

People ask me where I’m from.
Sometimes I wonder if I still belong anywhere.

I speak carefully, shaping every word to sound right.
But the language that used to feel natural now hides behind my tongue.

I send messages across time zones.
If I were home, I could just walk downstairs and knock on a friend’s door.

They say studying abroad is freedom.
But freedom often feels like distance in disguise.

At night, I look out at the city lights.
And I wonder if the person who never left home is looking at the same sky.
